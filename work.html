Convolution Reverb Project

A before and after with a recording of me saying hello to my friend, Tom. Please see Github link above for code.


In June 2022, I completed a 5-week work-term for a Maker Space based out of Sydney, Cape Breton. Their goal was to create a "practice microphone" that would plug into an I/O and have headphones out. The I/O was to be programmed with multiple effects such as delay, distortion, and compression, which could be switched on and off as the user pleased. I was tasked with creating the reverb effect for the device.

Convolution reverb consists of an impulse sample (a small recording of a clap in any room would do) and an input source merged together to artificially create the reverb of said room. My project is in C++ and I worked off of an old repo the head engineer had. During my term, I researched how to break down audio files in C, fourier transformations, and digital signal processing. In the end, I merged many open-source audio processing libraries together to acheive the end goal. The project took me the full 5 weeks to complete, finishing on the final day.
